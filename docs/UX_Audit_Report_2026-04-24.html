⚠️ Critical Finding: Landing Page Is a Login Form

/ (Root — aumtech.ai) 🚨 Critical
  • The homepage IS the login page. A first-time visitor landing on aumtech.ai is immediately presented with a login form — no product explanation, no value proposition, no "what is this?" context. This is the #1 UX anti-pattern for a marketing/product site.
  • Prospective buyers (university administrators, procurement teams) will bounce immediately. There is nothing to read, no demo, no "learn more."
  • The page title is "Aura | Your Campus Co-Pilot" but a visitor has no idea what Aura does before being asked to sign in.
  • No social proof (logos, testimonials, user count) is visible anywhere on the landing experience.
  • The "Forgot password" link exists but there is no "Request Access" or "Contact Sales" escape hatch for new prospects.
  • SEO meta tags are properly set. JSON-LD structured data exists.

💡 Recommendation

Create a proper marketing homepage at / with a hero headline, product screenshots or demo video, trust badges, and clear CTAs ("Request Demo" / "Sign In"). Move the login to /login. This single change will dramatically improve conversion from cold traffic.

Per-Page Findings

Platform / Architecture
aumtech.ai/architecture Needs Work
  • Animated SVG dataflow diagram is visually impressive and unique.
  • Hero typography (gradient eyebrow + large h1) is professional and consistent.
  • The diagram is cut off at the bottom — no visible end/footer boundary. Users don't know if there is more content below.
  • No explanatory text below the diagram. The diagram shows "SIS → PostgreSQL → API Gateway → LLM Engine → Aura App" but a non-technical buyer won't understand what this means without labels or a legend.
  • No CTA anywhere on this page. After viewing the architecture, what should a visitor do? There is no "Request Demo," "Learn More," or "Contact Us" prompt.
  • Page title in browser tab still reads "Aura | Your Campus Co-Pilot" — should be "Platform Architecture | Aura."
About / Company
aumtech.ai/about Good
  • Strong "Built by Practitioners" narrative — clear differentiator.
  • Gradient hero typography is polished and consistent with the brand.
  • Content reads as one long text block on mobile. No iconography or visual breaks in the body text.
  • Team/leadership section is missing. Institutional buyers want to know who is behind the product.
  • No CTA at the bottom of the page. This is a missed conversion opportunity after building trust with the company story.
Security
aumtech.ai/security Needs Work
  • FERPA / SOC 2 / HIPAA compliance claims are visible — builds trust.
  • Hero layout consistent with other pages.
  • Content appears in bullet-point prose but lacks visual hierarchy — no icons, no callout boxes, no diagrams to reinforce security claims.
  • Claims like "enterprise-grade encryption" have no evidence — no certifications, no auditor logos, no links to reports. Institutional IT teams will require this.
  • The word "HIPAA" appears in the footer badge but HIPAA doesn't typically apply to student academic data. This could raise red flags with compliance officers.
Support / Ethics Hotline
aumtech.ai/support Good
  • Three support cards now side by side — clean and scannable.
  • FAQ accordion adds interactivity and reduces cognitive load.
  • "Ethics Hotline" label in the footer links to the Support page — this conflation is confusing. Ethics hotlines are for reporting misconduct, not for tech support.
  • The contact form is dark navy on a light page — stark contrast break. Consider matching the form card to the page style.
  • No phone number or live chat. "Aura Voice AI Integration in Progress" is not confidence-inspiring for an enterprise buyer seeking support SLAs.
Careers
aumtech.ai/careers Good
  • Two-column layout makes excellent use of screen real estate — fits in one viewport.
  • Culture grid (Mission-Driven, AI-First, etc.) is compelling and honest.
  • "No Open Positions Right Now" is far better than "Page Coming Soon."
  • Email CTA (careers@aumtech.ai) links to a mailto — consider a short form instead so submissions don't get lost in email.
Privacy Policy / MSA / SLA
aumtech.ai/privacy · /msa · /sla Good
  • Two-column layout is an elegant use of space — sticky branding on left, scrollable content on right.
  • Content is concise and readable — not buried in legalese.
  • Privacy Policy mentions "Google Gemini AI" by name — ensure this is disclosed in your actual data agreements with partner institutions.
  • MSA and SLA are very brief for enterprise contracts. Institutional legal/procurement teams will want more detail or at least a "full contract on request" CTA.

🌐 Global / Cross-Page Issues

  • Browser tab title is the same on every page — "Aura | Your Campus Co-Pilot." Each page should have a unique title (e.g., "Security | Aura," "Architecture | Aura"). This hurts SEO and tab management.
  • No social proof anywhere — no university logos, no testimonial quotes, no "X students use Aura" counter. Institutional buyers need this before they trust you.
  • No demo video or screenshots of the actual product on any public page. Buyers cannot evaluate the product without requesting access.
  • Footer scrolls away on long pages (fixed in this session for public pages, but Login.jsx still has the old layout). Double-check the authenticated dashboard footer too.
  • "Aura" vs "Aumtech.ai" brand confusion — the product is called Aura, the company is Aumtech. This dual branding is inconsistent. The logo says "AURA," the domain is aumtech.ai, the footer says "Aura Academic Intelligence." Pick a primary brand name and stick to it.
  • No "Request Demo" CTA in the top navigation — the highest-value conversion action is absent from the one element visible on every page.
  • Mobile hamburger menu exists but secondary nav items (Sign In, Back) disappear — a mobile user cannot sign in without the hamburger.
  • FERPA compliance messaging is consistent across footer badges — good trust signal.
  • Inter font and indigo/pink gradient color system is distinctive and premium.
  • Scroll-to-top on page navigation is implemented correctly.

📋 Honest Verdict

What's Working

The visual design system (cream background, indigo/pink gradient eyebrows, large headings, Inter font) is polished and consistent. The animated architecture diagram is genuinely impressive and differentiating. The legal and compliance pages are clean and well-structured. The Careers page is a great example of content-rich single-screen design.

What Needs Urgent Attention

The site currently functions as an internal tool portal, not a marketing website. A university CIO landing on aumtech.ai sees a login form — and leaves. The single most impactful change is building a proper marketing homepage. Until that exists, all the beautiful interior pages are effectively invisible to the people who need to evaluate and buy the product. Everything else (social proof, CTAs, product screenshots, per-page titles) is secondary but should follow quickly.
